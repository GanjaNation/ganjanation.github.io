South Lake Tahoe: Council Considers Cannabis Tax/Fee Changes (Nov 2025)

Tight summary of the agenda item to reduce the retail cannabis tax and eliminate the Public Safety Mitigation Fee, plus fiscal impact and context.

What’s on the agenda

  • Reduce the retail cannabis tax from 6% to 3% (operators had asked for 1%).
  • Eliminate the Public Safety Mitigation Fee:
    • $18,750 per business, or $26,250 if cultivation is involved.
  • The Public Safety License Fee ($1,190/year) is noted but not flagged for removal in the text provided.

Current revenue picture

  • City collects ~$900,000/year in cannabis tax revenue from four licensed businesses.
  • Since May 2021, up to 50% of cannabis tax revenue is allocated to a community benefit grants program for nonprofits and City priorities.

Fiscal impact of the proposed changes

ChangeImpact
Cut retail cannabis tax from 6% → 3% ≈ $450,000 annual revenue loss
Eliminate Public Safety Mitigation Fee ≈ $82,500 additional annual loss
Total estimated reduction ≈ $532,500 / year

Consequences likely include a smaller community grants pool, pressure on infrastructure/public services, and the police officer position partially funded by the mitigation fee needing backfill from reduced tax revenue.

Process & votes

  • Only three councilmembers will vote: Robbins, Roberts, Jinkens.
  • Cody Bass must recuse; one seat is vacant.
  • Meeting stream/participation page: cityofslt.gov/…/Watch-City-CouncilCommission-Meetings

How we got here (condensed)

  • 2018 cannabis ordinance took months; a ~1,400-signature petition stalled it (led by Cody Bass, then a medical dispensary owner). A revised ordinance followed; zoning changes later allowed cultivation at his site.
  • 2021: Council dedicated up to 50% of cannabis tax revenue to community grants.
  • 2022 — Measure G: Voters (62.95%) replaced community benefit fees with a formal cannabis/professions tax (up to 6% on retail/distribution/manufacturing; $20/sq ft canopy for cultivation).
  • Jan 2024: City ended development agreements; Measure G tax structure remained.
  • Sept 2025: Operators argued the burden is too high; asked 6% → 1%. Staff brought back a 3% option.

What it means

For operators

  • Relief Lower tax rate and removal of a large mitigation fee improves margins and survivability.

For residents & nonprofits

  • Trade-offs Smaller grant pools; some services (e.g., policing, infrastructure) may need alternative funding or see reductions.
